Core ideas & conclusions

Bitcoin Fundamentals & Conviction

  • Bitcoin managed by a competent user is risk-free long-term; all else including T-bills carry risk.
  • Third party intermediaries will increasingly fail - self-custody is non-negotiable.
  • Bitcoin deletes the institution of central banking and the money printer.
  • Most cant beat Bitcoin’s CAGR by definition — unless you have a true edge, just hodl.

Debt Strategy & Leverage

  • Fiat-denominated unsecured debt is a short-dollar position that can grow one's BTC stack.
  • Statistically timing debt with bitcoin is difficult but can massively accelerate stack growth.
  • Credit is approved based on gamable attributes not real economic fundamentals.
  • The limiting constraint is total credit extended to a given SSN/entity.

Wealth as % of the Pie

  • In a fixed-supply system, your stack size = your lifetime wealth ceiling.
  • Consumption erodes your share; net productivity to humanity increases it.
  • Once fully in BTC, you must create/capture value or your share shrinks relative to others.

Competitive Reality in a Hyperbitcoinized World

  • Bitcoin + Starlink + AI = global equalization of opportunity.
  • Competition is planet-wide, including ultra-hungry, low-cost, high-output talent.
  • The head start window is closing; now is the time for asymmetric action.

Preparation & Morality

  • Hyperbitcoinization will erase safety nets; merit-based survival will be brutal.
  • Build capital buffers, skills, and networks before the fiat system collapses.
  • Bitcoin only rewards the hodler irrespective of how they got the coin.
  • Expect political backlash, scapegoating, and regulatory attacks — be sovereign.

Life Alignment

  • Align major decisions the Bitcoin trajectory (career, residence, citizenship).
  • Avoid fiat entanglements that can be used against you (VC, KYC etc).
  • Model all major moves in Excel; intuition is wholey insufficient for finance.

Practical Warnings

  • ETFs, custodial platforms, and paper BTC carry systemic and counterparty risk.
  • Legal and political systems will adapt slowly — don’t assume fairness or consistency.
  • Default assumption is leverage will destroy your wealth unless you calculate otherwise.
